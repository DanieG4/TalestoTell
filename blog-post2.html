Sipping Serenity in Kamakura

Kamakura is a small city just south of Tokyo. With massive historical significance Kamakura marks the turn of the tides in Japan when the samurai class rose to power. Kamakura holds a mix of historical landmarks, Buddhist temples and shrines and many scenic nature trails. I could truly go on for days about all that was seen on my visit, but that will come in time. For today, I want to tell you about the Tokeiji Temple- otherwise known as the Bamboo forest shrine.

Tokeiji Temple has a truly rich history, one that I feel proud to tell you about. It was founded in 1285 but Hojo Masako during a time where women had very limited rights. Many women at the time were forced to stay in unhappy, abusive and dangerous situations and marriages. This temple was built as a safe haven for these women to go to.
